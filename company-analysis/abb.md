ABB — Focused Company Brief

1) Core business and strategic focus
Question: What are ABB's primary product lines and strategic priorities?
Answer: Electrification, robotics & discrete automation, industrial automation, and motion (power & automation products). Strategic priorities: digitalization (software + automation), services, and decarbonization. Expand ABB once: Asea Brown Boveri.

2) Customer segments & use cases
Question: Which industries and buyer personas does ABB target, and what are the top use cases?
Answer: Utilities, industrial manufacturers, process industries (oil & gas, chemicals), buildings, data centers. Use cases: grid modernization, factory automation, robotic assembly, energy-efficiency retrofits. Buyer personas include plant/ops engineers, automation architects, and grid operators.

3) Product & platform architecture
Question: How do ABB products compose and integrate?
Answer: Field hardware (sensors, drives, PLCs/RTUs) + edge controllers/gateways + on‑prem or cloud services (analytics, fleet management). Integration via standard industrial protocols and APIs; compact message contracts often use Protocol Buffers (Protobuf) or OPC UA (Open Platform Communications Unified Architecture).

4) Standards, protocols & security
Question: What integration standards should you expect?
Answer: OPC UA, MQTT, Modbus, Ethernet/IP, Protobuf/gRPC for services; security via TLS, mutual auth, and role-based access controls.

5) Deployment models
Question: On‑prem, hybrid, or cloud?
Answer: Many ABB control products are on‑prem; analytics and fleet services increasingly hybrid or cloud. Air‑gapped OT (operational technology) environments and strict network segmentation are common constraints.

6) Pricing and procurement
Question: How are ABB offerings typically sold?
Answer: Hardware as CapEx, software via subscription or maintenance contracts, services billed per engagement. Procurement cycles are typically long and involve PoCs and vendor qualification.

7) Open‑source and SDKs
Question: Does ABB publish SDKs or connectors?
Answer: Some SDKs and partner connectors exist; availability varies by product. Expect vendor SDKs and partner-provided adapters for proprietary hardware.

8) Regulatory & compliance
Question: Which regulations matter?
Answer: Utilities and grid customers follow NERC/NA and IEC standards; data privacy may require GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) compliance in the EU and local privacy laws elsewhere.

9) Intellectual property and patents
Question: Are there IP constraints to watch for?
Answer: Industrial automation often contains patented control or hardware designs. Early diligence should surface any proprietary protocols or protocol patents.

10) Where to find technical artifacts
Question: What artifacts to request from ABB?
Answer: Architecture diagrams, API schemas (Protobuf), sample telemetry, firmware update processes, and deployment manifests (Kubernetes/Helm) for cloud-facing products.
